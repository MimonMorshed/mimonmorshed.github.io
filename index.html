About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in the WAVCIS Lab at Louisiana State University, working under Dr. Chunyan Li to understand how the ocean responds to extreme weather events.

My research focuses on surface current dynamics during cold fronts using High-Frequency (HF) radar data from the Gulf of Mexico. I analyze how atmospheric forcing temporarily overwhelms tidal patterns, producing rapid current acceleration and flow reversals.

Previously, I developed deep learning models for oceanic front detection in the Bay of Bengal using CNN-based semantic segmentation—automating what was traditionally a manual, time-intensive process.

Current Research

HF Radar Observations of Cold Front Impacts on Shelf Circulation

2024 – Present Gulf of Mexico

Using High-Frequency radar surface currents to examine atmospheric cold front impacts on the Louisiana-Mississippi continental shelf. During the March 2021 event:

  • Current speeds increased by 3–4×, exceeding 40 cm/s
  • Surface flow directions reversed within 12–24 hours
  • Along-shelf velocity showed the strongest response
  • Meteorological forcing temporarily overwhelmed tidal control
  • Normal circulation patterns recovered over ~5–7 days

Deep Learning for Oceanic Fronts Detection

2021 – 2023 Bay of Bengal

Developed a CNN-based semantic segmentation model to automatically detect oceanic fronts in the Northern Bay of Bengal. The model achieved high IoU scores and outperformed traditional gradient-based methods.

  • Created annotated dataset of 1,096 SST images (2018–2021)
  • Trained deep learning models (U-Net, SegNet, DeepLabV3+, PSPNet, etc.)
  • Automated detection vs. manual methods (saves hours of analysis time)
  • Enables real-time monitoring for environmental applications
