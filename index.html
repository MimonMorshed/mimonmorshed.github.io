Current Research

HF Radar Observations of Cold Front Impacts on Shelf Circulation

images/hfradar-fig1.jpg
600×450px

Figure: Surface current velocity patterns showing flow reversal and acceleration during cold front passage in the northern Gulf of Mexico.

Using High-Frequency radar surface currents to examine atmospheric cold front impacts on the Louisiana-Mississippi continental shelf. Analysis of the March 2021 event revealed rapid current acceleration (3–4× increase), complete flow reversal within 12–24 hours, and temporary suppression of tidal patterns. This research quantifies how synoptic-scale meteorological forcing temporarily dominates tidal control of shelf circulation.

Deep Learning for Oceanic Front Detection

images/mldetection-fig2.jpg
600×450px

Figure: Automated oceanic front detection using semantic segmentation—input SST, ground truth, and CNN predictions for the Bay of Bengal.

Developed CNN-based semantic segmentation models for automatic detection of oceanic fronts in the Bay of Bengal using satellite observations. The deep learning approach achieved high IoU scores and outperformed traditional gradient methods, enabling real-time environmental monitoring and reducing analysis time from hours to seconds.
