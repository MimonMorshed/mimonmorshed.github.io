Overview

Atmospheric cold fronts are synoptic-scale weather systems that frequently traverse the Gulf of Mexico during fall and winter months. This research investigates how these meteorological events influence continental shelf circulation, with particular focus on shelf response mechanisms, transport processes, and ecological implications.

Research Questions

  • How do cold front passages modify surface and subsurface current patterns on the shelf?
  • What are the timescales of shelf response to atmospheric forcing events?
  • How does front intensity and propagation speed affect circulation changes?
  • What are the implications for coastal transport and marine operations?

Methodology

Observational Data

• HF Radar: Surface current measurements from WAVCIS network
• Meteorological Data: Wind speed, direction, and atmospheric pressure from NOAA stations
• Satellite Observations: Sea surface temperature and wind fields
• In-situ Measurements: ADCP profiles and CTD casts from field campaigns

Analysis Approach

Cold front events are identified using meteorological criteria (wind shifts, pressure changes). For each event, we analyze:

• Pre-front, during-front, and post-front circulation patterns
• Response timescales and spatial extent of shelf adjustments
• Correlation between front characteristics and circulation changes
• Composite analysis across multiple cold front events

Preliminary Results

Initial Observations

  • Rapid Response: Shelf currents respond within 6-12 hours of cold front passage
  • Current Reversal: Strong westward to eastward current reversals observed during events
  • Spatial Variability: Response magnitude varies with water depth and distance from shore
  • Recovery Time: Post-frontal circulation patterns persist for 2-4 days before returning to pre-front conditions

Ongoing Work

  • Statistical analysis of multiple cold front events across multiple seasons
  • Development of predictive framework for shelf response to atmospheric forcing
  • Manuscript preparation for Journal of Physical Oceanography
